# Vision & Mental Model

## What Is an Engram

An engram is a living knowledge organism. Not a notebook. Not a database. Not a folder of files. It is a self-organizing, self-healing body of knowledge that digests raw sources into structured, interlinked articles, and grows smarter every time you use it.

Users create engrams for different domains of their life — a research engram, a startup engram, a personal engram, a project engram. Each evolves independently. Each can be private, shared with a team, or published to the world. Cross-engram queries let you synthesize across boundaries.

The system does the organizing. You feed it and interrogate it. That's it.

## Core Principles

**Zero-organization overhead.** Users never create pages, folders, links, or tags manually. The LLM does all structuring, linking, and indexing. Feed raw material in, get organized knowledge out.

**Compounding usage.** Every query makes the engram smarter. Other tools treat queries as throwaway. In Engrams, they're first-class knowledge that gets filed back into the wiki, creating new articles and connections.

**Living knowledge.** Background agents mean your engram evolves when you're not using it. Stale information gets refreshed. Gaps get flagged. Connections get discovered.

**Universal ingestion.** One click, one paste, one forward, one share, one drag. Plus continuous sync from 20+ services. No other tool makes getting knowledge in this frictionless across this many surfaces.

**Full provenance.** Every fact traces to a source. Every change is versioned. Trust is architectural, not aspirational.

**Data ownership.** Everything is local-first markdown, git-versioned, exportable. You always own your data.

## Brand Posture

Engrams occupies the intersection of scientific instrument and beautiful object. Think Leica cameras, Braun calculators, Teenage Engineering synthesizers. Products where the engineering is the aesthetic, where restraint is the luxury, where every surface says "this was made by people who care about craft."

The product should feel like something a researcher at the Santa Fe Institute would use. Or a writer at a residency. Or a founder building in public. It doesn't dumb anything down. It doesn't add tutorials unless asked. It trusts the user. It rewards depth.

**Competitive posture:** Obsidian is a toolbox. Notion is a spreadsheet wearing makeup. Engrams is a living organism. We don't compete on features. We compete on the feeling of having a second mind that actually works.

## What Makes This Unbeatable

1. **Zero-org overhead.** The LLM handles all structuring. Impossible for Obsidian/Notion to replicate without a full rebuild.
2. **Compounding usage.** Every query enriches the wiki. Substantial answers become synthesis articles. Discovered gaps feed research. New connections update the map.
3. **Living knowledge.** Background agents refresh stale info, detect gaps, discover connections, and synthesize large clusters — all while you sleep.
4. **Feed it anything, from anywhere.** Browser extension, drag-and-drop, paste, share sheet, email forwarding, voice memo, screenshot OCR, CLI, API, plus 20+ service integrations with continuous sync.
5. **Multi-engram with cross-pollination.** Specialized knowledge organisms that can be queried across boundaries.
6. **Full provenance.** Every fact traces to a source. Every compilation pass is logged. Every change is a git commit you can revert.
7. **You own your data.** Markdown in a git repo. Export anytime. No lock-in.
8. **It's beautiful.** The map is something you want to stare at. The reader is somewhere you want to spend hours. The confidence heatmap makes you want to explore your own knowledge.
9. **It has a soul.** The vocabulary, the voice, the posture — it feels like a place where thinking happens, not software you tolerate.
